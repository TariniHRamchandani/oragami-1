Camel

  • A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back. Camels have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food and textiles.
  • Lifespan: Dromedary: 40 years
  • Scientific name: Camelus

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons or chamaeleons are a distinctive and highly specialized clade of Old World lizards with 202 species described as of June 2015. These species come in a range of colors, and many species have the ability to change color.
  2. Scientific name: Chamaeleonidae
  3. Higher classification: Acrodonta
  4. Family: Chamaeleonidae; Rafinesque, 1815
